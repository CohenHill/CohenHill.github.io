First Tech Challenge

what is Ftc

FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) is an international robotics competition for students, where teams design, build, and program robots to compete in dynamic, real-world inspired challenges. Using engineering principles, custom hardware, and programming in languages like Java, FTC participants collaborate in a high-pressure environment that mirrors professional tech projects.

What I did

I competed in FTC from 2023 to now as Software lead for my High School team.  

why it matters

Participating in the FIRST Tech Challenge goes far beyond building a robot—it cultivates a well-rounded set of professional and interpersonal skills. 

                                    Through FTC, I learned how to:                                    

· Solve complex technical problems under real-world time constraints using engineering principles

· Collaborate effectively in diverse teams with clearly defined roles and shared project goals

·  Program functional, real-world robotics systems using industry-standard languages like Java

· Present confidently in both formal pitch settings and public outreach initiatives

· Mentor new and younger FTC teams by sharing technical knowledge and guiding their development

First Robotics Challenge

what is FRc

FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) is a global, high-level robotics challenge where high school teams design, build, and program industrial-grade robots to complete complex tasks on a dynamic game field. With strict time constraints, large team sizes, and real engineering demands, FRC mirrors the pace and collaboration of real-world product development and systems engineering.

What I did

I competed in FRC from 2025 to now as a Software member for a Community Robotics Team

why it matters

Being part of FRC means more than building an impressive robot—it’s about taking on real responsibility, solving tough problems, and engaging with the world beyond the competition. 

                                    Through FRC, I learned how to:                                    

· Design and build large-scale mechanical systems that balance durability, efficiency, and precision

· Program advanced robot control systems using tools like WPILib, Java/C++, and sensor integration

·  Manage full project lifecycles—from brainstorming and prototyping to final execution

· Collaborate with professionals, sponsors, and other teams to share resources and ideas

· Promote STEM in the community through outreach events, demos, and mentorship programs

Boy Scouts

what is Boy Scouts

The Boy Scouts of America (BSA) is one of the nation's most respected youth leadership and character development organizations. Through a structured program of outdoor skills, service, advancement, and leadership, Scouts develop real-world capabilities that extend far beyond camping trips.

What I did

As a Life Scout, a member of the Order of the Arrow, and the Senior Patrol Leader (SPL) of a large troop, I held one of the most demanding youth leadership roles available in Scouting. 

why it matters

                            Through Boy Scouts, I learned how to:                                    

· Lead a large, diverse group of peers through weekly meetings, camping trips, and major events

· Organize and delegate responsibilities across multiple patrols and junior leaders

·  Mentor younger Scouts and help guide them through rank advancement and personal growth

· Plan and execute complex activities—from multi-day campouts to troop meetings

· Work toward Eagle Scout, demonstrating sustained commitment, initiative, and leadership in service
